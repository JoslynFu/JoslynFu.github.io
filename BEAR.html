Research Assistant
at BEAR

Founded in 2014, Berkeley Economic Advising and Research (BEAR) is a California professional partnership dedicated to quality economic analysis. Individually and together, we have advised national and regional governments in over 50 countries, a broad array of nongovernmental and enterprise institutions around the globe, and many multilateral and bilateral institutions.

I joined Professor David Roland-Holst's research team at Berkeley Economic Advising and Research (BEAR), where I was involved with a World Bank sponsored project on the co-benefits of carbon pricing. I collaborated in a team-setting and took on the aspect of transportation and technology change to conduct literature review and economic modeling analysis. For instance, I simulated a flow chart on quantifying the economic impacts of reduced road injury from data on gasoline tax and price, road injury, GDP loss and property damage. I also examined the empirical regression analysis, which produces country specific estimates for road fatality elasticities from changes in gasoline price.

In addition to this project, my interest in environment and data analytics also led me to join another project on researching annual stocks and flows of biomass by category and location. I built a biomass inventory for California through collecting data sets from various data sources and plotting a spatial distribution of biomass resources using RStudio by making stacked bars using geospatial data. My work builds the basis for a statewide model of biomass accounting and process modeling, and I look forward to learning more about data science in the field of renewable energy.
