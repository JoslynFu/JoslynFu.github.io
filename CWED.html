Research Assistant
at CWED

Under the Institute for Research on Labor and Employment (IRLE), Center on Wage and Employment Dynamics (CWED) is nationally recognized for its trailblazing research on the impact of minimum and subminimum wage policies, as well as the effects of the Great Recession, public sector workers, low-wage labor markets, and teacher pay.

As the assistant to Professor Michael Reich, I researched the intersection of racial inequality and minimum wages by investigating whether minimum wage policies reduce the racial wage gaps. Using a multivariate reweighting method to reweight the black wage distribution and applying multi-way fixed-effect regression models in Stata, I found that minimum wage increases are not race-neutral. In addition, I implemented counterfactual simulations to estimate the white and black worker hourly wage gap in different scenarios. My findings, such as the estimation that the gap would be increased by 14 percent without minimum wage policies, reinforced our conclusion that minimum wage increases continue to be a powerful tool for ameliorating racial inequality.
